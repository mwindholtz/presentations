The results …

Previous studies by Maximilien and Williams observed Test First Development achieved a 50% improvement in code quality without any significant impact on productivity.   The results of the current study found that the Test-First group achieved on average over 70% better productivity and code quality and the improvement rose to 80% for the medium to high skilled group of programmers.

 

The number of test methods was written was a highly statistically significant predictor of both quality and productivity, whereas skill level was not.  The amount of test assets is a significant predictor of quality and productivity.
